Including Sub-Tracks

HMDP, MMDP, LMDP

Description

Microsatellites, also known as Short Tandem Repeats (STRs), form distinct density landscapes across the human genome. These landscapes represent the relative density of STRs per kilobase along the complete reference Human Genome GRCh38.

STRs tend to cluster in characteristic patterns, resulting in pronounced microsatellite density peaks distributed throughout the genome. These peaks can be further classified into three categories:

  • High Microsatellite Density Peak (HMDP)
  • Middle Microsatellite Density Peak (MMDP)
  • Low Microsatellite Density Peak (LMDP)

Thus, the primary track includes four sub-tracks:

  • STR Density Landscape
  • HMDP
  • MMDP
  • LMDP

Methods

The GRCh38 genome was divided into 1kb resolution units (bins) using a differential method. The local microsatellite relative density in each 1kb unit, referred to as Position-related Differential-unit 1kb (pD1), was calculated with the following formula:
